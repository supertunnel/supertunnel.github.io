Biography

Dr. Liu obtained his doctorate from Tongji University. During the doctoral research, he worked on geomechanics problems in tunneling activities, under the supervision of Prof. Zixin Zhang. Dr. Liu engaged in several tunnel projects in China during his doctoral research, including Shanghai Yangtze River Tunnel (D=15.43 m), West Changjiang Road Tunnel (D=15.43 m), Qiantang River Tunnel (D=15.43 m), and Beijing Underground Cross City Railway (BJUCR) Tunnel (D=13 m), etc.

During his stay in the U.S., Dr. Liu spent most of his efforts on geomechanics related numerical simulations using multiple approaches, i.e. FEM, DEM, CFD and coupled DE-FE method. The DEM code Ellip3d, as well as its parallel version paraEllip3d (using hybrid OpenMP and OpenMPI ), was adopted in the research on tunnel face failure process in cobble and sand strata. The two codes are initially developed by the research group of Professor Richard Regueiro at University of Colorado, Boulder. Dr. Liu participated in the codes development during his stay in Colorado (09/2012~09/2013).

After his doctorate, Dr. Liu joined the research group directed by Dr. C. Y. Kowk at HKU as a postdoctoral researcher. During the severn-months research, Dr. Liu worked on two topics: (i) CFD-DEM modeling of debris flow, using the opensource code CFDEM; and (ii) grain-based modeling of granite, using the commercial software PFC2D.

Currently, Dr. Liu serves as an Associate Professor (tenured) at Guangzhou University, directing the geomechanics and geohazards research group. Dr. Liu's research interests are the applications of numerical approaches in both geotechnical engineering and geological process. Recently, he obtained the funding from NSFC, Guangdong-NSF, and Guangzhou University, supporting the research in multiscale modeling for the soil responses during the failure process in tunneling.
